    Partners Dave Chan and Dave Hom originally opened Makana "to fill our own appetites," and eventually realized they might make some money off the deal.

    The native New Yorkers ate a lot of Hawaiian food while living on the West Coast.

    "When we came back, we noticed there wasn't anything like it," said Mr. Chan. "At least nothing like we experienced out there."

    Makana's Manhattan Valley outpost opened in September. The expansion from East Harlem is in keeping with the business plan, Mr. Chan said: "Go into up-and-coming neighborhoods and grow with them," adding that there is beginning to be a bit of "a restaurant scene" in the new spot's neighborhood.

    The Makana menu offers monster meals such as the Loco Moco, ground-beef patties with caramelized onions, mushroom gravy, garlic and a fried egg (served with a side for $7.95 at lunch). The Kalua pulled pork, available as a sandwich or a plate (both $7.95), is another favorite, Mr. Chan said.

    The loaded fries ($6.95) come with the sweet pork, cheese, onions and toasted sesame -- don't order it without a group of friends.

    Spam, the "filet mignon of Hawaii," is featured heavily on the menu, from the house fried rice ($10.95) with Spam, shrimp, pineapple, egg and peas, to the Spam avocado roll ($5) offered on Makana's full sushi menu.

    Makana, 161 W. 106th St. near Amsterdam Avenue; opens daily at 11:30 a.m.; 212-678-4569.
